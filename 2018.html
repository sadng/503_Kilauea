East Rift Zone

May to September 2018


On April 30th, 2018, the Kilauea crater collapsed, ending the long lived Pu‘u‘ō‘ō eruption. Days later on May 3rd, 2018, lava broke the surface along the lower East Rift Zone. This was the start of Kilauea's 2018 eruption. On September 5th, 2018, this eruption had its last active flow of lava visible throughout fissure 8.

Fissure vents throughout the lower East rift zone erupted viscous lava flows throughout Leilani Estates, a residential subdivision. Mid-May, lava flows began traveling faster and further, as the magma started becoming hotter and more fluid. In late May, the Kilauea caldera continued to collapse as the eruption slowly drained its magma chamber. Throughout this eruption, a total of 62 collapse events occurred. Over 8,700 acres was covered by lava, with 875 of those acres being new land created due to the eruption. Over 700 structures and 30 miles of road were covered and/or inaccessible due to blocked access.
